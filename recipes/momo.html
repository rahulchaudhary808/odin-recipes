Momo is a popular Tibetan and Nepali dumpling filled with various ingredients such as vegetables, meat, or a combination of both. They are typically served with a dipping sauce. Here's a basic recipe for making vegetable momos:

Ingredients:

For the dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • pinch of salt
  • Water, as needed

For the filling:

  • 2 cups finely chopped or grated vegetables (cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, etc.)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon soy sauce (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vinegar (optional)

For the dipping sauce:

  • 1/2 cup tomato ketchup
  • 2-3 tablespoons chili sauce
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the dough:
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and a pinch of salt.
    • Gradually add water and knead the dough until it's smooth and pliable. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling:
    • Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
    • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Saut√© for a minute or until fragrant.
    • Add chopped onions and cook until they turn translucent.
    • Add the finely chopped or grated vegetables and cook for a few minutes until they are soft.
    • Season with salt and black pepper.
    • You can also add soy sauce and vinegar if desired.
    • Let the filling cool completely.
  3. Assemble the momos:
    • Divide the rested dough into small, equal-sized balls and roll them into thin circles, about 4-5 inches in diameter.
    • Place a spoonful of the vegetable filling in the center of each circle.
  4. Fold and shape the momos:
    • Fold the circle in half, creating a half-moon shape.
    • Pinch the edges together to seal the momo. You can create pleats along the edges to make them look attractive.
  5. Steam the momos:
    • Place the momos in a greased steamer and steam for about 15-20 minutes or until the momos are cooked through.
  6. Prepare the dipping sauce:
    • Mix tomato ketchup, chili sauce, minced garlic, soy sauce, and salt in a bowl to make the sauce.
  7. Serve the momos hot with the dipping sauce.

Enjoy your homemade momos! You can also experiment with different fillings, such as ground meat or other vegetables, to create your favorite variations.
