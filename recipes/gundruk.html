Ingredients:

  • Prepared gundruk (fermented leafy greens)
  • Vegetable oil
  • Onion, sliced
  • Garlic, minced
  • Green chilies, chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Turmeric powder, a pinch (optional)
  • Water, as needed

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Gundruk: If you have dried gundruk, soak a small portion in water for about 15-20 minutes until it softens. Rinse it a couple of times to remove excess salt. Squeeze out any excess water.
  2. Heat Oil: Heat a pan or skillet over medium heat and add a couple of tablespoons of vegetable oil.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Add the sliced onions and minced garlic to the hot oil. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
  4. Add Green Chilies: Toss in the chopped green chilies and sauté for a minute or two. Adjust the amount of chilies to your desired level of spiciness.
  5. Add Gundruk: Add the prepared gundruk to the pan. Stir-fry for a few minutes, allowing the gundruk to absorb the flavors of the aromatics.
  6. Season: Season the gundruk with salt to taste. Be mindful of the salt as gundruk can be salty due to the fermentation process. You can add a pinch of turmeric for color if you like.
  7. Add Water: If the gundruk appears too dry or you want a bit of gravy, you can add a small amount of water. Start with a few tablespoons and adjust as needed. This will create a flavorful broth.
  8. Simmer: Cover the pan and simmer the gundruk for about 5-10 minutes, or until it is cooked to your preferred level of tenderness.
  9. Taste and Adjust: Taste the gundruk and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You can add more salt or chilies according to your taste.
  10. Serve: Once the gundruk is cooked and the flavors are well-incorporated, remove it from the heat.
  11. Serve: Serve the cooked gundruk as a side dish with steamed rice or as a complement to other Nepali or South Asian dishes.
